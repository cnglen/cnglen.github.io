AB Test

  • \(H_0\): NULL hypothesis,
    • a particular treatment has no effect
    • there is no difference between the controlled(version A) and treatment(version B) group
  • \(H_1\): alternative hypothesis
  • Type I Error, False Positive, \(P(\hat{H_1}|H_0 \text{ is true}), \alpha\), believing a lie, false alarm,
  • type II error, Flase Negative, \(P(\hat{H_0}|H_1 \text{ is true}), \beta\), faling to raise an alarm
  • Power: \(1-\beta\)

A random sample of \(n\) observations \(X_i, i=1,2,\cdots,n\) is taken from a normal populatoni with mean \(\mu\) and variance \(\sigma^2\).

  • sample mean \(\bar{X}=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}X_i\)
  • sample variance \(S^2 = \frac{1}{n-1} \sum_{i=1}^{n} (X_i - \hat{X})^2\)

Hypothesis

  • Control Group: \(X_0 \sim (\mu_0, \sigma_0^2) \Longrightarrow \bar{X_0} \sim N(\mu_0, \frac{\sigma_0^2}{n_0})\)
  • Treatment Group: \(X_1 \sim (\mu_1, \sigma_1^2) \Longrightarrow \bar{X_1} \sim N(\mu_1, \frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1})\)
  • \(H_0: \mu_1-\mu_0=0\)
  • \(H_1: \mu_1-\mu_0 = \delta \neq 0\)
  • \(n_0 = n_1 = n\)

Then,

\begin{equation} 0 + z_{1-\frac{\alpha}{2}}\sqrt{\frac{\sigma_0^2}{n}+\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n}} = \delta - z_{1-\beta}\sqrt{\frac{\sigma_0^2}{n}+\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n}} \end{equation} \begin{equation} n = \frac{ (z_{1-\frac{\alpha}{2}}+z_{1-\beta})^2 (\sigma_0^2+\sigma_1^2)}{\delta^2} \end{equation}

Hypothesis

  • Control Group: \(X_0 \sim (\mu_0, \sigma_0^2) \Longrightarrow \bar{X_0} \sim N(\mu_0, \frac{\sigma_0^2}{n_0})\)
  • Treatment Group: \(X_1 \sim (\mu_1, \sigma_1^2) \Longrightarrow \bar{X_1} \sim N(\mu_1, \frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1})\)
  • \(H_0: \mu_1-\mu_0=0\)
  • \(H_1: \mu_1-\mu_0 = \delta > 0\)
  • \(n_0 = n_1 = n\)

Then,

\begin{equation} 0 + z_{1-\alpha}\sqrt{\frac{\sigma_0^2}{n}+\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n}} = \delta - z_{1-\beta}\sqrt{\frac{\sigma_0^2}{n}+\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n}} \end{equation} \begin{equation} n = \frac{ (z_{1-\alpha}+z_{1-\beta})^2 (\sigma_0^2+\sigma_1^2)}{\delta^2} \end{equation}
