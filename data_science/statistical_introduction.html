Bias Variance trade-off

Assuming:

  • \(y = f(\mathbf{x}) + \epsilon\)
  • \(E[\epsilon] = 0\) and \(\epsilon\) is independent of \(\mathbf{x}\)

We use \(\hat{f}(\mathbf{x})\) estimate \(y\).

For an observation \((\mathbf{x_j}, y_j)\) in test set: \(E[(\hat{f}(\mathbf{x}_j) - y_j)^2] = E[(\hat{f}(\mathbf{x}_j) - f(\mathbf{x}_j) - \epsilon_j)^2] = E[(\hat{f}(\mathbf{x}_j) - f(\mathbf{x}_j))^2] + E[\epsilon_j^2]\)
